Getting Here

Your Path to Mfangano Island

Mfangano Island sits in the Winam Gulf of Lake Victoria, accessible only by boat from the mainland. Whether you are travelling from Nairobi or Kisumu, the journey is part of the adventure — and well worth it.

From Kisumu: Fly into Kisumu International Airport (KIS), where regular domestic flights operate via Safarilink and Airkenya Express. From Kisumu, take a short connecting flight to Mbita Airstrip, or drive approximately 2–3 hours through scenic western Kenya countryside to Mbita or Homa Bay town. The route follows the A109 and A1 highways with beautiful views of the highlands descending toward the lake.

From Nairobi: Direct flights to Kisumu are available from Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (NBO). From there, follow the Kisumu route above. Alternatively, overnight buses and self-drive options cover the approximately 350 km journey in 5–6 hours.

The Lake Crossing

Sena Ferry Mfangano Island

From Mbita, the regular passenger and vehicle ferry crosses to the Sena landing point on Mfangano Island. The crossing takes approximately 45 minutes to 1 hour and runs on a published schedule throughout the day.

Smaller private boats are also available for hire at Mbita jetty if you prefer a more direct crossing or arrive outside ferry hours.

Once you arrive at Sena Ferry Dock: Kadongo Cool Breez Hotel Resort is just 100 metres away along the road to St. Linus Catholic Church. Look for our welcoming sign or call us from the ferry — we will gladly come to meet you and assist with your luggage.

Transportation

Ways to Reach Us

Multiple routes lead to Mfangano Island. Choose the option that suits your travel style.

✈️

By Air

Fly into Kisumu International Airport (KIS) from Nairobi's JKIA. Connecting flights via Safarilink and Airkenya Express operate to Mbita Airstrip, bringing you close to the lake shore.

🚗

By Road

From Kisumu, drive 2–3 hours through scenic western Kenya countryside to Mbita or Homa Bay town. Private cars, taxis, and matatu (minibus) services are all available along this route.

⛴️

By Ferry

From Mbita, cross to Sena on Mfangano Island via the regular vehicle and passenger ferry. The crossing takes approximately 45 minutes to 1 hour and runs on a published daily schedule.

Explore the Island

Nearby Attractions

Mfangano Island is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. Our team can help arrange visits to these remarkable places.

🎨

Rock Art Sites

Discover ancient rock paintings at Mawanga, believed to be between 1,000 and 4,000 years old. A site of deep spiritual significance, historically linked to rainmaking rituals.

🏛️

Historical Shrines

Visit sacred shrines scattered across the island, each holding centuries of cultural and spiritual heritage of the Abasuba community and their ancestral traditions.

🐟

Fishing Villages

Experience the daily rhythm of traditional fishing communities. Watch local fishermen bring in their catch and learn about time-honoured fishing techniques.

🏖️

Lake Victoria Beaches

Relax on the serene shores of Africa's largest lake. Enjoy sunset views, boat rides, and the calm beauty of the Winam Gulf's tranquil waters.

🎭

Cultural Heritage Sites

Explore the Abasuba Peace Community Museum and discover traditional artifacts, storytelling, and guided cultural tours celebrating the island's rich heritage.

🥾

Nature Walks

Traverse the island's lush trails through indigenous vegetation, enjoy panoramic viewpoints, and encounter diverse birdlife in this natural paradise.
